Basic Types of Stitches

Chain

Every project starts with a chain stitch!

It's important to keep your tension even while creating your chain, as this will affect the overall look of your project. The length of your chain determines the width of your project.

Slip Stitch

The 'Braid-Like' Stitch

The slip stitch may be the "easiest" type of stitch, but will take longer to complete projects. It grows slowly, takes a lot of patience, and can be harder to determine where the end of your project is to start off a new row.

Single Crochet Stitch

The Most Commonly Used Stitch

This is another simple stitch that most beginners will start with. It's always helpful to watch beginner videos on Youtube or other blog pages to get a better understanding of how to create these stitches.

Double Crochet Stitch

Single Crochet's Taller Cousin

The double crochet stitch is a bit taller than the single crochet, creating a looser fabric. It's great for projects that need a bit more drape and flexibility.
